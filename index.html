Open-World Scene Graph Generation for Retrieval-Augmented Navigation

Fei Yu1
1Zhejiang lab
Paper arXiv Code

Abstract

Understanding 3D scenes is a fundamental task in computer vision and robotics, yet existing methods are limited by their reliance on predefined datasets and closed-vocabulary annotations, making them unsuitable for dynamic, open-world environments. To address this, we propose Open-World Scene Graph Generation for Retrieval-Augmented Navigation, a framework designed to enhance 3D scene understanding and interactive exploration. Our approach tackles the challenges of open-world scenarios by integrating Vision-Language Models (VLMs) and retrieval-augmented techniques.

The framework consists of two key components: (1) a scene graph generation module that dynamically captures objects, predicates, and relationships without fixed annotations, and (2) a retrieval-augmented navigation system that enables interactive query-based exploration. By leveraging open-vocabulary classification and spatial relationship modeling, our method achieves competitive performance on benchmarks like 3DSSG and Replica. We validate the framework through three distinct navigation tasks, demonstrating its robustness and scalability. Experimental results show significant improvements over existing methods, highlighting its effectiveness in generating accurate 3D scene graphs and enabling retrieval-augmented navigation in complex environments. This work advances 3D scene understanding by providing a scalable and adaptive solution for open-world applications.

Overall framework of the Open-World 3D Scene Graph Generation for Retrieval-Augmented Navigation. Our framework consists of two core components: (1) Scene Graph Generation, which constructs a semantic and spatial representation of the 3D environment by detecting objects and their relationships; and (2) Retrieval-Augmented Navigation, which supports three types of queries: (i) spatial object information queries, (ii) object semantic relation queries, and (iii) instance queries for locating specific instances within the scene. This integrated approach enables robust and context-aware navigation in open-world environments.
